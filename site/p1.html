So you have learned that water is virtually not compressible. Fine, that isn't life-changing or whatever. However, what about if water is truly wet? If it isn't, what exactly causes YOU to feel wet when you touch water?! According to sciencefocus.com, water by itself is not wet. However, how hydrophobic (or water-repelling) a surface it comes into contact with largely determines how "wet" that substance feels as water comes into contact with it. Thus, oils and wax paper will "feel" not-really-wet if they could have feelings, while humans' water-attracting skin largely causes us to suffer in misery when get soaked in a sudden unexpected thunderstorm.
